## Tactical Doctrine & Capabilities

**Primary Role:** Field army for operations in Western Desert. Primary mission: relieve Tobruk garrison and destroy Axis forces in Cyrenaica (Operation Crusader, November 1941).

### Special Capabilities

- Combined arms mobile warfare (armoured and infantry corps coordination)
- Desert operations expertise (7th Armoured Division veteran)
- Fortress relief operations (Tobruk)
- Air-ground coordination (RAF Western Desert Air Force)
- Large-scale logistics over extended supply lines
- Commonwealth integration (British, Australian, New Zealand, South African, Indian, Polish formations)

### Tactical Innovations

- Army-level command structure for desert warfare (first time)
- Integration of American equipment (Stuart M3 tanks, Grant tanks arriving Q4)
- Improved tank-infantry cooperation tactics (lessons from Battleaxe)
- Enhanced artillery coordination (Forward Observation Officers with armoured units)
- Tobruk garrison as base for offensive operations (rather than passive siege)

### Known Issues

- Crusader tank mechanical reliability poor (many breakdowns during training)
- Inexperienced formations (50th Division, 2nd SA Division) lacking desert expertise
- Command and control at army level still developing (first major operation)
- 2-pounder anti-tank guns marginal vs German Panzer III/IV
- Tank tactics inferior to German combined arms methods (tank-heavy vs combined arms)
- Supply lines extended (250km+ from Alexandria to frontier)
- Commander (Cunningham) inexperienced in desert mobile warfare (East Africa background)

**Desert Adaptations:** Enhanced water storage and purification, improved vehicle air filters, desert camouflage, specialized desert rations, extensive spares stockpiling, mobile workshops, forward repair facilities, sun compass navigation, leaguer defensive tactics, night movement procedures, heat acclimatization training for new units.
